4. Cookies & Tracking Technologies

Our website may use cookies, web beacons, pixels, and similar technologies (collectively, “cookies”) to collect and store certain information when you visit or interact with our site.

4.1 What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are placed on your device by a web server when you visit a website. They allow the website to recognize your device, remember your preferences, and understand how you use the site.

4.2 Types of Cookies We May Use

  • Essential cookies: These cookies are necessary for the website to function properly and enable core features such as page navigation and access to secure areas.
  • Performance and analytics cookies: These cookies help us understand how visitors use our website by collecting information such as which pages are visited and how long users stay on each page.
  • Functional cookies: These cookies allow the site to remember choices you make (such as your region or language) and provide enhanced, more personalized features.
  • Advertising or targeting cookies: Where used, these cookies may be set by us or by third-party partners to deliver relevant advertising or measure the effectiveness of our marketing campaigns.

4.3 Your Choices About Cookies

Most web browsers are set to accept cookies by default, but you can usually modify your browser settings to remove or reject cookies. Please note:

  • Disabling certain cookies may impact the functionality or performance of our website;
  • You may need to adjust cookie settings on each device and browser you use;
  • Some third-party advertising networks may offer opt-out mechanisms for targeted advertising.

For more information about cookies and how to manage them, refer to your browser’s help documentation or visit industry resources that explain cookie controls.
